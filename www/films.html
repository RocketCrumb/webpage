Trespass is a film I made in partnership with Southeastern Railway. The idea behind the film was to inform people (specifically school kids) about the
dangers crossing a train track. Me and my 5 fellow pupils from my Media Studies class collabarativly worked on the project (I edited it independantly).
We were invited kindly by them to film on one of their offline railway tracks and provided us with some train instructiors and police staff to interview.
They were happy with the outcome of the project and most importantly my edit and now use it to show around schools as a informative message.

Don't get me started on this film. I made this for my EPQ (Extended Project Qualification). The topic I chose to explore was how the effects of suicide can effect us mentally.
I sourced all my references and researched as much as I could about cinema conventions and tropes to give as much of an unbiased, inoffensive film as I possibly could.
I had always had this idea that i wanted to encorporate a familar fairy tale into a film so much that you couldn't tell it was the same story until someone pointed out that
this film is based off of x, and then in that moment their whole perspective of the film, theme and fairy tale would change. Its always been a pipedream of mine and this is
what I based the film and script off. I filmed it and even edited it and it came out just the way I had envisioned but then tragedy struck. The starring actor wanted the film
